🗓️ 시험 구조 한눈에
파트별 시간과 특징
▾
Part 1 — 4~5분
Examiner가 familiar topics (본인, 집, 취미, 루틴)에 대해 질문. 짧고 자연스럽게 대답. 너무 길게 말하면 오히려 감점.
Part 2 — 3~4분
Cue card 받고 1분 준비 후 1~2분 혼자 말하기. 구조가 명확해야 함. 준비 시간 활용이 핵심.
Part 3 — 4~5분
Part 2 주제와 연결된 추상적 질문. 의견 + 이유 + 예시 구조로 깊이 있게 답변.
🇳🇿 뉴질랜드 영어 특징
시험관이 쓰는 표현들
▾
IELTS 시험관이 뉴질랜드 억양이라도 당황하지 마. 주요 특징:

발음 차이:
• "fish and chips" → "fush and chups" 처럼 /ɪ/ → /ʊ/ 발음
• "ten" → "tin" 처럼 들릴 수 있음
• 문장 끝을 올리는 억양 (rising intonation) 자주 씀

뉴질랜드식 표현들:
Sweet as / Choice
= Great, awesome (뉴질랜드 슬랭)
"Sweet as, bro!" — 시험에서 쓰면 안 되지만 시험관이 쓸 수 있음
Keen
= Interested, eager (영국식 영어에서 자주 쓰임)
"Are you keen on outdoor activities?" — 이런 질문 나올 수 있음
Heaps
= A lot, many (뉴질랜드·호주식)
"There are heaps of things to do in my city." — 답변에 써도 자연스러움
Cheers
= Thank you (영국·뉴질랜드식)
시험 끝날 때 "Cheers for your time" 가볍게 써도 OK

▾

Q: Do you like living in your hometown?
I'd say I have mixed feelings about it, to be honest. On one hand, I really appreciate the convenience — everything I need is within easy reach. But on the other hand, it can feel a bit overwhelming at times because it's such a fast-paced city. I think I'd enjoy living somewhere quieter eventually.

Cue Card — 1분 준비, 2분 말하기
⏱️ 1분 준비 시간 활용법
이걸 못 쓰면 절반은 날리는 거야
▾
1분 준비 루틴
1
0~15초: 카드 전체 읽기 — Who/What/Where/When/Why 파악
2
15~40초: 4개 bullet point마다 키워드 1~2개씩 메모 (영어로)
3
40~60초: 첫 문장(intro) 머릿속으로 연습 + 마지막 wrap-up 생각
💡 메모는 단어만! "trip / Jeju / family / summer / relaxing" 이 정도면 충분. 문장 쓰다가 시간 낭비하지 마.
🗺️ WWWWW 스피킹 구조
2분을 채우는 확실한 공식
▾
WWWWW Formula
W
Who — 누구와? 혼자? 가족? 친구?
W
What — 무엇을? 구체적 묘사

✈️ 카드 예시 1 — A memorable trip
▾
Cue Card: Describe a trip you enjoyed.
You should say: where you went / who you went with / what you did there / and explain why you enjoyed it.
모범 답안 (Band 6.5~7)
I'd like to talk about a trip I took to Jeju Island a couple of years ago with my family. It was during the summer holidays, and I was really looking forward to it because I'd been under a lot of pressure from work and desperately needed a break.

We spent about five days there, and we made the most of every day. We hiked up Hallasan, which was breathtaking — the views from the top were absolutely stunning. We also visited some of the black lava beaches, which I'd never seen before, and tried loads of local food like black pork and fresh seafood.

What made it really special was just being able to switch off completely from everyday life. There were no deadlines, no responsibilities — just good food, beautiful scenery, and quality time with my family. I remember thinking I wouldn't have changed it for the world.

Looking back, I think it was one of those trips that recharges you in a way that nothing else can. I'd love to go back someday.
분석: 시간 순서로 자연스럽게 흐름. 감각적 묘사(breathtaking, stunning). 이디엄 3개 자연스럽게 삽입. 마지막에 감정 wrap-up.
👤 카드 예시 2 — A person you admire
▾
Cue Card: Describe a person you admire.
You should say: who this person is / how you know them / what they do / and explain why you admire them.
모범 답안
The person I'd like to talk about is my high school English teacher, Mrs. Kim. She taught me during my final two years of school, and she was without doubt one of the most influential people in my life.

What set her apart was the way she made learning feel genuinely exciting. She didn't just go through the motions like some teachers do — she really invested in her students. I remember she used to stay after class to help anyone who was struggling, never making you feel embarrassed for not understanding something.

I admire her because she led by example. She was always well-prepared, passionate about her subject, and genuinely cared about our future. Because of her, I developed a real love for the English language, which is actually why I'm here taking this exam today.

I think great teachers leave a lasting impression, and she's definitely someone I'll always be grateful for.
🏠 카드 예시 3 — A place you'd like to visit
▾
Cue Card: Describe a place you would like to visit.
You should say: where it is / why you want to go / what you would do there / and explain why it interests you.
모범 답안
A place I've always had my heart set on visiting is New Zealand, particularly the South Island. I've seen so many photos and documentaries about it, and it just looks absolutely spectacular.

The main reason I want to go is the natural scenery — the fjords, the mountains, the crystal-clear lakes. Places like Milford Sound look like something straight out of a movie, and I'd love to experience that in person. I'd also want to try adventure activities like bungee jumping or skydiving, since New Zealand is renowned for that kind of thing.

Another thing that draws me there is the culture. New Zealand has a really unique blend of Māori heritage and modern life, which I find fascinating. I'd love to learn about Māori traditions and maybe even attend a cultural ceremony.

I think it would be the trip of a lifetime, and it's definitely on my bucket list.

🌍 토픽 1 — Travel / Tourism
▾
자주 나오는 질문
• Why do people enjoy travelling?
• Do you think tourism is always beneficial for a country?
• How has the way people travel changed in recent years?
• Should governments invest more in tourism?
모범 답안
Q: Do you think tourism is always beneficial for a country?
Not necessarily. While tourism undoubtedly brings significant economic benefits — boosting local businesses and creating jobs — it can also have some negative consequences that are easy to overlook.

For instance, popular destinations like Bali or Barcelona have been overwhelmed by tourists in recent years, which has led to rising living costs for locals and serious environmental damage. This phenomenon is often called "overtourism."

That said, I think the key is sustainable management. If governments strike the right balance between attracting visitors and protecting local communities, then tourism can absolutely be a positive force. So it's not a black-and-white issue — it really depends on how it's managed.
💻 토픽 2 — Technology & Society
▾
자주 나오는 질문
• How has technology changed the way we communicate?
• Do you think people rely too much on technology?
• What are the negative effects of social media on young people?
• Will AI replace human jobs in the future?
모범 답안
Q: Do you think people rely too much on technology?
I think there's a strong case to be made for that. Technology has become so deeply embedded in daily life that many people — myself included — would struggle to function without it. We use devices for everything from navigation to communication to entertainment, and that level of dependency is worth questioning.

However, I'd argue it's not necessarily a bad thing if the reliance is purposeful. The problem arises when technology takes over activities that are better done without it — like face-to-face conversations or spending time in nature. There's a risk of people losing touch with basic human skills.

So I think the real issue isn't technology itself, but how consciously we use it. It's a tool, and like any tool, the impact depends on who's using it and how.
🎓 토픽 3 — Education
▾
자주 나오는 질문
• What makes a good teacher?
• Should education be free for everyone?
• How important is university education today?
• Should children be taught life skills at school?
모범 답안
Q: How important is university education today?
It's still quite important, but I think its role is evolving. Traditionally, a degree was seen as the golden ticket to a successful career. But nowadays, with the rise of online learning and vocational training, there are more alternative pathways available.

That being said, for many professions — medicine, law, engineering — a university degree remains essential. And beyond qualifications, university provides valuable experiences like critical thinking, independence, and networking.

I suppose my view is that university is valuable but not the only route. What matters most is finding a path that aligns with your strengths and goals. The idea that everyone must go to university is a bit outdated, in my opinion.
🌿 토픽 4 — Environment
▾
자주 나오는 질문
• Who is more responsible for environmental problems — individuals or governments?
• What can ordinary people do to help the environment?
• Should governments ban plastic packaging?
• Why do some people ignore environmental issues?
모범 답안
Q: Who is more responsible — individuals or governments?
I think it has to be both, but if I had to pick one, I'd say governments bear greater responsibility. Individuals can make changes like recycling or reducing plastic use, but these efforts are relatively small-scale compared to the impact of industrial pollution and poor policy-making.

Governments have the power to legislate and enforce meaningful change — things like carbon taxes, renewable energy targets, and emissions regulations. Without that kind of structural action, individual efforts can only go so far.

That said, I don't think individuals are off the hook entirely. Consumer choices drive demand, and collective behaviour does create pressure on both businesses and governments to act. So really, it's a shared responsibility.
